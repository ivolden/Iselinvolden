Going Nowhere

I'm an old man, driving far stretched roads - going nowhere. And one day a young boy stopped to ask me:

You live so free, you do what you want, but my fears they hinder me to do the same. What if i lost everything, even the clothes off my back?

And i answered:

You go to find some food and water. You get up and feel the breeze against your skin and dirt beneath your feet.

You pass a tree and a rabbit might pass you. You smell the flowers and hear the birds.

Eventually you find a well and drink, you cloth yourself and taste various fruits.

You grow older and your experience change. You keep walking, feeling the dirt beneath your feet, the rain on your skin.

You meet all sorts of people, funny ones, pretty ones, mean ones, strange ones. You tell them stories and they tell you theirs.

Then you die. You compose and worms eat you. They poop and a tree absorb you, grows its leaves and life goes on, just in a different way. No more smells, no more tastes. (Enjoy them while you can)

Then a moose eats the leaves and poops them out. The poop dries up and a kid eats them to impress his school mates because his dad told him that you can eat dried up moose shit, and life goes on.

then the world goes under, your poop particles melt, collide with other particles and forms a new sun that burns for billions of years, and life goes on, just in a different way.
